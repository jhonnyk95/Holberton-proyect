A good time to remember those games that have shared great adventures in our childhood.

The Games:

In 40 years of videogame history, there are so many themes, genres and formats that there are titles for all tastes. But there are some that, we like more or less, have been especially important for the industry. The British newspaper The Guardian has made a list of the five video games of the 80s and 90s most influential in history, perhaps the years of greatest proliferation of this form of entertainment.

The most revelant:

Pac-Man (1980)

Without a doubt, one of the most famous 80s and 90s video games in history. So much so that the yellow kite has become a symbol of the arcade world. And not only that, also in an icon of nostalgia. Fame came from the beginning, as it achieved the guinness record by being the best selling recreational those years.

The secret of Pac-Man's success was, among other things, the game's own theme. For the first time, some humor is presented and it is not just about killing, killing and finishing spaceships. That made them want to play women and men of all ages and, hence, they sold many machines and became the video game of the 80 most successful of that time and that time.

Super Mario Bros (1985)

Little can we say that this video game of the 80s is not known anymore. From this one, or from any of the titles starring the most famous plumber in the world that continued to emerge in the 90s and 2000s. The first one by Mario Bros (1983) and Donkey Kong's were the first platforms ... hence we understand Your influence With Super Mario Bros, the great Shigeru Miyamoto consolidated the biggest symbol of Nintendo.

Doom (1985)

Like most of the most influential video games of the 80s and 90s, this also introduced a novelty. In his case, it was the fact that it was the first title in the first person. That is, the player lived the story in the first person, something unthinkable until that moment.

The protagonist is a marine who, in full routine mission, encounters a failure in a teleportation experiment that opens the gates of hell. And from there come a lot of demons and evil spirits that change in zombies all the fallen Marines who are in their path. The player is the only surviving human being and his goal is to remain so.

GoldenEye 007 (1997)

It is a first person shooter and spy video game developed by Rare and published by Nintendo for its Nintendo 64 console in 1997. The title is based on the 1995 James Bond movie of the same name. It is the post-James Bond title: The Duel, released for several Sega platforms in 1993. It was followed by James Bond 007, released for Game Boy in 1997.

The game received mostly positive reviews, and sold more than eight million copies. The work was praised for the quality of its multiplayer mode and the modification of stealth elements and varied objectives in its single-player missions, in contrast to the classic mechanics of the FPS of simply shifting the level by eliminating enemies. He is quite regarded as one of the best first-person shooters of all time1 and as the best title in the Bond saga.

MegaMan X (1993)

MegaMan X, known as Rockman X, is a video game developed in 1993 by Capcom, it is the first video game in the Mega Man X series. Mega Man X is the first subsaga of Mega Man, and was created primarily as the stepping stone to progress from the Mega Man video games of the NES to those of the Super Nintendo.

The game was originally released in 1993 for Super Nintendo, published by Capcom in the United States and Japan, and by Nintendo in Europe. In 1995, the game was published for PC.
